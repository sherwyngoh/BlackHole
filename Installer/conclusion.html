Privacy & System Access

When you first launch Clarify, macOS may request permission for system event control. This is required for Clarify to:

  • Process audio in real-time - Receive and monitor continuous audio streams from your microphone
  • Manage audio device events - Detect when audio devices connect, disconnect, or change
  • Route audio through the virtual device - Coordinate audio flow between applications and the BlackHole driver
  • Optimize voice enhancement - Apply advanced AI audio processing to improve your voice clarity

This permission is essential for voice enhancement to function. Clarify does not use this access for any other purpose.

FAQ

Where is the Clarify Mic located?

The Clarify Mic is a virtual audio device. You can find it in Audio MIDI Setup and within audio applications for use as a microphone input.

How can I listen to the audio while using Clarify?

Set up a Multi-Output Device to monitor your audio while processing through Clarify.

Why is nothing audible through Clarify?

  • Check System Preferences -> Security & Privacy -> Microphone to make sure your audio application has microphone access.
  • Check that the volume is all the way up on Clarify Mic output in Audio MIDI Setup.
  • If you are using a Multi-Output Device, due to issues with macOS a 2ch audio driver must be enabled and listed as the top device in the Multi-Output.

How do I uninstall Clarify?

You can uninstall Clarify through System Settings or by using the uninstall script provided with the application.
